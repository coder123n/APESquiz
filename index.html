Vocabulary by Chapter

Chapter 1

tragedy of the commons

If anything is shared with no rules, the positive effect is individual but the negative impact is shared. Individuals will rationally take all the benefit, which causes the resource to degrade.

sustainability

using renewable resources at a rate at which they can fully replenish themselves(ex. reproduce, regrow)

megacities

urban areas with a population of at least 10 million (used to be 8 million)

unsustainability

using unrenewable resources or using resources too quickly for it to come back/make more (replenish)

carrying capacity

the maximum amount of life that can be supported by an environment without lowering the capacity in the future

precautionary principle

if there is any threat to the environment, we should immediately work to avoid possible damage (better safe than sorry)

environmental valuation

the way we value our environment based on 8 reasons (justifications):
utilitarian(benefits economically), ecological(necessary for ecosystem), asthetic, recreational, inspirational, creative, moral, cultural
