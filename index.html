AP Environmental Science Quiz

Preparing for an APES test? We have lots of questions here perfect for studying key vocabulary that you will need to know for your test!

Vocabulary by Chapter

Chapter 1

tragedy of the commons

If anything is shared with no rules, the positive effect is individual but the negative impact is shared. Individuals will rationally take all the benefit, which causes the resource to degrade.

sustainability

using renewable resources at a rate at which they can fully replenish themselves(ex. reproduce, regrow)

mega cities

urban areas with a population of at least 10 million (used to be 8 million)

unsustainability

using nonrenewable resources or using resources too quickly for it to come back/make more (replenish)

carrying capacity

the maximum amount of life that can be supported by an environment without lowering the capacity in the future

precautionary principle

if there is any threat to the environment, we should immediately work to avoid possible damage (better safe than sorry)

environmental valuation

the way we value our environment based on 8 reasons (justifications):
utilitarian(benefits economically), ecological(necessary for ecosystem), asthetic, recreational, inspirational, creative, moral, cultural

Chapter 2

Environmental science is NOT like other sciences. Science is NOT about belief, they do not go together!!

observation

something seen, heard, felt, or touched (experienced by the 5 senses)

inference

an assumption made based on observations

fact

a statement that can be proved and is not based on belief

hypothesis

a prediction of an outcome

dependent(responding) variable

the outcome, based on another variable

independent(manipulated) variable

the cause of the outcome

quantitative data

number values; quantity

qualitative

non number values

controlled experiment

compared to a control without the independent variable

theory

thoughts/ideas to explain something that is backed up by considerable evidence

scientific model

a simple construction of a complex system to test a complicated system

scientific method

a structure of science based on observation, measurement, hypothesis, and testing

pseudoscience

something that looks like science, but is not science

science

a way to understand believable phenomenon

"layers"(types) of science

knowledge - tested and proved, generally agreed upon
frontier - advancing knowledge, not fully accepted, not yet proved
fringe - father "out there", little data/evidence, not accepted
beyond the fringe - WAY "out there", based on values/beliefs, not supported by observation, can not be proved

Chapter 3

natural capital

the ecological systems that perform public service functions

public service functions

when ecosystems sustain renewable resources without hman interaction
examples: forests absorb particles, wetlands & organic soils treat sewage, filtering water

environmental economics

persuading people to control pollution/ environmental damage and sustain renewable resources
sustainability pollution, environmental damage, renewable resources

polluter-pay system

whoever creates the pollution pays for it (individual cost - solution to TOTC)

a commons

land (or another resource) owned publicly, with public access for private uses
examples of environmental commons: cow grazing, fishing, forests, atmosphere/air, ocean, national parks

external(indirect) costs

not recognized by producers -> doesn't show in the price
examples: fishing whales- loss of revenue to whale-watching boats, loss of ecological role of whales in the ocean

internal(direct) costs

those costs had by the producer in obtaining, processing, and distributing a product -> passed directly to the user/consumer
ex. buying a pencil- external: degradation of environment surrounding factory, mining for metal/rubber internal

pseudoscience

something that looks like science, but is not science
