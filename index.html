AP Environmental Science Quiz

Preparing for an APES test? We have lots of questions here perfect for studying key vocabulary that you will need to know for your test!

Vocabulary by Chapter

Chapter 1

tragedy of the commons

If anything is shared with no rules, the positive effect is individual but the negative impact is shared. Individuals will rationally take all the benefit, which causes the resource to degrade.

sustainability

using renewable resources at a rate at which they can fully replenish themselves(ex. reproduce, regrow)

megacities

urban areas with a population of at least 10 million (used to be 8 million)

unsustainability

using unrenewable resources or using resources too quickly for it to come back/make more (replenish)

carrying capacity

the maximum amount of life that can be supported by an environment without lowering the capacity in the future

precautionary principle

if there is any threat to the environment, we should immediately work to avoid possible damage (better safe than sorry)

environmental valuation

the way we value our environment based on 8 reasons (justifications):
utilitarian(benefits economically), ecological(necessary for ecosystem), asthetic, recreational, inspirational, creative, moral, cultural

Chapter 2

Environmental science is NOT like other sciences. Science is NOT about belief, they do not go together!!

observation

something seen, heard, felt, or touched (experienced by the 5 senses)

inference

an assumption made based on observations

fact

a statement that can be proved and is not based on belief

hypothesis

a prediction of an outcome

dependent(responding) variable

the outcome, based on another variable

independent(manipulated) variable

the cause of the outcome

quantitative data

number values; quantity

qualitative

non number values

controlled experiment

compared to a control without the independent variable

theory

thoughts/ideas to explain something that is backed up by considerable evidence

scientific model

a simple construction of a complex system to test a complicated system

scientific method

a structure of science based on observation, measurement, hypothesis, and testing

pseudoscience

something that looks like science, but is not science
